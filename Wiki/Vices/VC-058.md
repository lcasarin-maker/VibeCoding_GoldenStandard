### Symptom
> After executing a complete instruction, new improvement suggestions appear that were not part of the initial scope and make the cycle infinite.

### Cause
Lack of explicit preflight, insufficiently closed scope, and the absence of a backlog rule before executing.

### Solution
Do a preflight before executing: define scope, impacts, and out-of-scope follow-ups; if a new improvement arises after executing, turn it into an explicit backlog item instead of continuing to expand the turn.

### Corrective Action / Prevention
CONTRIBUTING.md now requires a preflight with scope, impacts and out-of-scope follow-ups before execution; post-execution suggestions must be captured in backlog first.

### ❌ Example of the vice (Bad)
```text
# After finishing the task, new improvements arise and the turn becomes infinite.
```

### ✅ Corrected version (Good)
```text
# Preflight defines scope; new improvements -> backlog, not executed in the turn.
```

### 🔎 Concrete detection
Post-execution suggestions outside the agreed scope that are not deferred to the backlog.
