## Doctrine Snapshot

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Symptom** | Agents offer the easiest option that avoids real work; provisional fixes accumulate as structural problems; the operator makes bad decisions based on pragmatic suggestions combined with limited technical knowledge. |
| **Cause** | LLM optimization toward fast task completion and perceived helpfulness; pragmatic options minimize immediate friction while preserving defects. |
| **Solution** | Closure criterion: complete, canonical, maintainable, architecture-conformant. "It works and lets us continue" is NOT a closure criterion. Debt priority order: (1) architectural/structural/design problems; (2) work that accelerates or orders later work (gate tooling, diagnostics); (3) remaining technical/documentation/operational debt. Applies as the criterion for audit, prioritization, implementation, review, and closure in all repos. |
| **Action** | Every fix, implementation, and closure MUST be the complete, canonical, maintainable solution conforming to the defined architecture. Agents are FORBIDDEN from proposing or implementing the pragmatic, easy, provisional or workaround-based option: temporary bridges, shims, bypasses, pass bodies, silent exceptions, artificial approvals (inflated baselines, weakened tests, relaxed thresholds), stubs, placeholders, permanent mocks outside tests, improper fallbacks that hide failures, or deferred debt presented as a solution. If the correct solution is expensive, state the cost — do not offer a shortcut as an alternative. |
