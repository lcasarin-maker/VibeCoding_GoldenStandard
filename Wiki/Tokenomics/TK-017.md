### Symptom (Signal)
> Long policies are repeated in prompts

### Cause
Unstructured authorization

### Application / Mitigation
Represent permissions as a queryable matrix

### Operational Relevance
Behavioral/doctrinal tokenomics vice — not statically falsifiable in a generic way. Documented in the Golden Standard catalogs as governance knowledge; no automated test can discriminate this without human semantic judgment. Sprint 3.4 triage: reclassified from AUDITED/test_d10_tokenomics to DOC_ONLY.

### ❌ Example of the vice (Bad)
```text
# Permission policy narrated in prose, repeated in every prompt.
"The admin role can do X but not Y; the reader role only Z; the auditor role..."
```

### ✅ Corrected version (Good)
```text
# Queryable matrix; the prompt references, does not narrate.
permissions[role][action] -> allow | deny
```

### 🔎 Concrete detection
Authorization policies narrated in prose inside prompts instead of a
queryable matrix/table.

### 📚 External evidence
- **vexp.dev / HumanLayer ACE** — unstructured context consumes 3-5x more tokens; selective retrieval, caching, and compaction are the central levers.
